HOT ROD DATABASE

The Site


The movie Hot Rod (2007), directed by Akiva Schaffer and starring Andy Samberg, is technically a bad movie. Although the humor is childish and the entire film relies a bit too heavily on slapstick comedy, it is one of me and my roommate's favorite films of all time. We reference it constantly, and enjoy subjecting our friends to repeated viewings with commentary (which simply consists of him and I saying the lines with the actors). The movie is a pivotal part of our friendship, so when Tim's (my roommate's) birthday came around I had a great idea. I would go through the movie, make gifs of a few of our favorite parts, then load them all up onto a flash drive and give it to him. It would be easy-peasy...
It wasn't easy-peasy

The Gifs

The first thing I needed were gifs. I was supposed to find only 10 to 15 or so gifs to collect, but I soon realized that there were way too many good moments to choose. What started as a 30-45 minute venture - take the clips, shorten them, and then make them into gifs - soon turned into a 15 hour sludge, as I made over 200 different gifs.


The Site

Once I had all of the gifs, I needed a place to put them. I quickly realized that a flash drive wasn't gunna cut it - if you wanted to actually use these gifs, they would have to be available on messaging devices. This is where the idea for a website came in. The site was simple, mostly governed by a javascript function that made adding / changing gifs very easy. I quickly figured out that a search bar was necessary in order to find a specific gif. This was actually a very simple thing to add in given the preexisting website structure. Once all of the gifs were loaded in, I made them show up in pages, so as to get rid of the awful strain it would put on the computer to run all 215 gifs simultaneously. However, as I soon learned, this would not be enough...


The Issues

I ran into many issues when I tried to put all of the gifs on the website, and because of this, I went through many different iterations of the site. The first thing I tried was starting with a blank site with no gifs, and then have javascript add in all of the gifs when the site loaded up.
    Pros: This makes adding new gifs easy as pie.
    Cons: The computer has a tough time loading in over 200 gifs at once.
I had to think of a different plan. Instead of having javascript load in all of the gifs at load time, why don't I just hardcode everything?
    Pros: Uh... I'll be honest this was a really bad idea.
    Cons: This didn't really help with the loading time like I thought it would...
I had to change my approach. The major problem I was having was that loading tons of gifs all at once made loading a pain. This gave me an idea: why don't I load them in one at a time? I would have the gif only load in when the user clicked on the gif's block.
    Pros: This drastically cut down on load time.
    Cons: It was kind of difficult to understand what gif you needed, now that the gif was not showing until you clicked on it.
It was close, but still not the perfect solution. Thankfully, the best solution simply required one more step: put thumbnails on all the gifs.
    Pros: The load time is minimal and the user can have a good idea of what gif they are looking at before they activate the gif.
    Cons: I now I have to go through and make thumbnails for ALL 215 GIFS. I THOUGHT I WAS DONE WITH THIS PROJECT.
